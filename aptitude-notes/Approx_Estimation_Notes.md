## 2. Word Problems (Linear Equations)

**Q1. The sum of two numbers is 36, and their product is 248. Find the sum of their reciprocals.**
**Ans:** Let numbers be $x$ and $y$.
$x + y = 36$
$xy = 248$
$\frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} = \frac{x+y}{xy} = \frac{36}{248} = \frac{9}{62}$

**Q2. In a poultry farm having hens & goats, Sneha can see 84 heads & 282 legs. How many hens & goats are there?**
**Ans:** Let Hens = $x$, Goats = $y$.
$x + y = 84 \quad \text{--- (1)}$
$2x + 4y = 282 \quad \text{--- (2)}$
Multiply (1) by 2: $2x + 2y = 168$
Subtracting from (2): $2y = 114 \implies y = 57 \text{ (Goats)}$
Substitute in (1): $x = 84 - 57 = 27 \text{ (Hens)}$

**Q3. Sum of 5 consecutive numbers is found to be 335. If we add the largest & smallest numbers, what will we get?**
**Ans:** Let numbers be $(x-2), (x-1), x, (x+1), (x+2)$.
Sum = $5x = 335 \implies x = 67$.
Numbers are 65, 66, 67, 68, 69.
Smallest + Largest = $65 + 69 = 134$.

**Q4. Om was not paying attention in class. Instead of multiplying by $\frac{3}{4}$, he divided by $\frac{3}{4}$. This led to a difference of 14 between the two answers. What is $x$?**
**Ans:** Dividing by $3/4$ is multiplying by $4/3$.
$\frac{4x}{3} - \frac{3x}{4} = 14$
$\frac{16x - 9x}{12} = 14 \implies 7x = 14 \times 12 \implies x = 24$

**Q5. Saish has 2 bags, A and B. If 10 pebbles from bag B are shifted to bag A, the number of pebbles become interchanged. But if 10 pebbles from bag A are put into bag B, then bag B has twice the number in A. How many pebbles do A and B have?**
**Ans:** *(Note: Corrected small inconsistencies from original notes to ensure algebraic soundness)*
$B - A = 20 \quad \text{--- (1)}$
$2(A - 10) = (B + 10) \implies 2A - 20 = B + 10 \implies 2A - B = 30 \quad \text{--- (2)}$
Adding (1) and (2):
$A = 50$
Substituting in (1): $B = 70$.
Bag A has 50 pebbles, Bag B has 70.

**Q6. A group wanted to renovate their club. Each member contributed an amount equal to twice the number of members. The government contributed the same amount as the number of members. If each member had contributed an amount equal to the number of members, and the government gave twice the number of members, they would have ₹210 less. How many members are there?**
**Ans:** Let number of members = $x$.
Case 1: Total = $x(2x) + x = 2x^2 + x$
Case 2: Total = $x(x) + 2x = x^2 + 2x$
Difference is 210:
$(2x^2 + x) - (x^2 + 2x) = 210$
$x^2 - x - 210 = 0$
$(x-15)(x+14) = 0$
Since members cannot be negative, $x = 15$.
