**Q1. Ratio of two numbers is 3:8. On adding 5 to both numbers the ratio becomes 2:5. Which is the smallest out of the two?**
**Ans:** Let numbers be $3x$ and $8x$.
$$\frac{3x+5}{8x+5} = \frac{2}{5}$$
$$5(3x+5) = 2(8x+5)$$
$$15x + 25 = 16x + 10 \implies x = 15$$
Smallest number = $3(15) = 45$.

**Q2. Divide 3395 in the ratio 42:32:23.**
**Ans:**
$$42x + 32x + 23x = 3395 \implies 97x = 3395 \implies x = 35$$
The parts are:
$42(35) = 1470$
$32(35) = 1120$
$23(35) = 805$

**Q3. If $A:B = 3:7$, $B:C = 9:5$. Find $A:B:C$.**
**Ans:**
Make B the same in both ratios:
$\frac{A}{B} = \frac{3 \times 9}{7 \times 9} = \frac{27}{63}$
$\frac{B}{C} = \frac{9 \times 7}{5 \times 7} = \frac{63}{35}$
$A:B:C = 27:63:35$

**Q4. The sum of three numbers is 285. Ratio of 1st & 2nd is 3:7. Ratio of 2nd & 3rd is 6:5. The third number is?**
**Ans:**
Combining ratios like above: $18:42:35$.
$$18x + 42x + 35x = 285 \implies 95x = 285 \implies x = 3$$
Third number = $35(3) = 105$.

**Q5. Ramesh & Suresh income ratio is 5:6. Their spending ratio is 7:9. Ramesh saves ₹4000 and Suresh saves ₹3000. What is the income of Ramesh & Suresh?**
**Ans:** Let expenditure be $7x$ and $9x$. 
*(Note: Income = Expenditure + Savings)*
Income of Ramesh = $7x + 4000$
Income of Suresh = $9x + 3000$
$$\frac{7x+4000}{9x+3000} = \frac{5}{6}$$
$$42x + 24000 = 45x + 15000 \implies 3x = 9000 \implies x = 3000$$
Ramesh Income = $7(3000) + 4000 = 21000 + 4000 = 25000$.
Suresh Income = $9(3000) + 3000 = 27000 + 3000 = 30000$.
*(Correction: Calculated total income directly instead of just expenditure).*

**Q6. If $\frac{10}{13} = \frac{11}{28} = \frac{21}{11} = k$. Find $k$.**
**Ans:** By theorem of equal ratios:
$$k = \frac{10+11+21}{13+28+11} = \frac{42}{52} = \frac{21}{26}$$

**Q7. What is the fourth proportion of 9, 13, 153?**
**Ans:** Let fourth proportion be $x$.
$$\frac{9}{13} = \frac{153}{x} \implies x = \frac{153 \times 13}{9} = 221$$

**Q8. Find mean proportion of 7, 63.**
**Ans:**
$$x^2 = 7 \times 63 = 441 \implies x = \sqrt{441} = 21$$

**Q9. Ratio of number of storybooks to non-storybooks in a library was 4:3. Total number of storybooks was 1248. Some more storybooks were bought and then the ratio became 5:3. Find the number of storybooks bought.**
**Ans:**
Initial storybooks $4x = 1248 \implies x = 312$.
Non-storybooks = $3x = 936$.
After buying, new ratio is 5:3.
Let new storybooks be $5y$. Since non-storybooks didn't change, $3y = 936 \implies y = 312$.
New storybooks = $5(312) = 1560$.
Books bought = $1560 - 1248 = 312$.

**Q10. ₹8400 is divided among A, B, C, D in such a way that the shares are in the ratio 2:3, 4:5, 6:7 respectively. The share of A is:**
**Ans:** Combine $A:B = 2:3$, $B:C = 4:5$, $C:D = 6:7$.
$$A:B:C:D = 16:24:30:35$$
Sum of parts = $16 + 24 + 30 + 35 = 105$.
A's share = $\frac{16}{105} \times 8400 = 1280$.

**Q11. The ratio of market price of wheat and rice is 2:3 and the ratio of expenditures consumed in a family is 5:4. Find the ratio of quantities of wheat & rice.**
**Ans:**
$\text{Expenditure} = \text{Price} \times \text{Quantity} \implies \text{Quantity} = \frac{\text{Expenditure}}{\text{Price}}$
Ratio of Quantities = $\frac{5}{2} : \frac{4}{3} = \frac{5}{2} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{15}{8}$.
*(Correction: Adjusted arithmetic to properly calculate the ratio of quantities).*

**Q12. Three numbers are in the ratio $\frac{1}{2} : \frac{2}{3} : \frac{3}{4}$. The difference between the greatest & smallest number is 36. Find the numbers.**
**Ans:** Multiply by LCM (12): Ratio is $6x : 8x : 9x$.
Greatest - Smallest = $9x - 6x = 3x = 36 \implies x = 12$.
Numbers are: $6(12) = 72$, $8(12) = 96$, $9(12) = 108$.

**Q13. If $a:b = 5:7, c:d = 20:36$, then $ac:bd$ is:**
**Ans:**
$$\frac{ac}{bd} = \frac{5 \times 20}{7 \times 36} = \frac{100}{252} = \frac{25}{63}$$
*(Correction: Reduced fraction to lowest terms for mathematical accuracy).*
